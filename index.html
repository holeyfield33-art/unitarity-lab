How to connect your HuggingFace model

Three paths from any HuggingFace model to a spectral audit. The notebook is the fastest start.

  1. Load your model in the starter notebook — Click Launch Gemma 4 Starter Notebook below. The notebook imports any HuggingFace model via AutoModelForCausalLM.from_pretrained("your/model-id"), attaches activation hooks, and computes hidden-state eigenvalues automatically.
  2. Run the coherence estimator — estimate_coherence_score(model, tokenizer, prompt) returns the k=1 Berry-Keating invariant and a GUE r-statistic proxy. Paste those numbers into the result panel below for tier classification.
  3. Interpret your numbers — Use the Foundational Methods cards below to decode what your k-value and r-statistic mean. For production-grade hidden-state and eigenvalue analysis, follow the links in the Deeper Analysis section.

Foundational Methods

Key reference parameters for spectral rigidity and coherence analysis.

Spectral Rigidity

GUE Ensemble Target

r ≈ 0.603

The GUE (Gaussian Unitary Ensemble) r-statistic measures the average ratio of consecutive eigenvalue gaps in the model's hidden-state weight matrices. Random Matrix Theory predicts r ≈ 0.603 for systems with time-reversal symmetry breaking — the same distribution observed in chaotic quantum systems.

  • r near 0.603: Eigenvalue spacing matches GUE prediction — normal spectral rigidity, no anomalous structure detected.
  • r < 0.53 (Poisson limit): Eigenvalue repulsion breaks down — possible layer collapse, weight degeneracy, or uncorrelated blocks. Investigate with hidden-state analysis.
  • r > 0.65: Stronger-than-expected eigenvalue repulsion — possible over-regularization, rank deficiency, or unusual training dynamics.
Coherence Invariant

Berry–Keating k=1

k = 1

The Berry–Keating k-value characterizes coherence structure in the activation manifold geometry. k=1 corresponds to first-order coherence — the model's latent representations form a smooth, well-conditioned geometry consistent with stable information flow across layers.

  • k = 1: First-order coherence intact. Latent geometry is well-conditioned. Model activations exhibit expected spectral correlations.
  • k = 0: No detectable coherence structure. Activations are uncorrelated — possible dead layers, catastrophic interference, or meaningless residual stream.
  • k > 1: Higher-order coherence detected. Can indicate strong geometric structure from fine-tuning, or — at high values — pathological entanglement between representations that should be independent.
